# 02 — Renno's Critique · Player Advocate

*Voice: Renno, cynical playtester. Has seen a thousand clever designs die in the first ten minutes of actual play.*

---

## Would I play this?

For about six minutes, yes. The bones are there. Mother as a real opponent is the right call, and the two-bar resource system gives me something to juggle. But there are three things that would make me quit before run two, and Maya needs to hear them plainly.

---

## Top Three Concerns

### 1. Three lose conditions is two too many for a game you're supposed to grasp in 30 seconds

Maya says the ISOLATION loss is "rare and always visible." Then it's not a real lose condition — it's a footnote that clutters the rules surface. I've playtested enough games to know what happens when you have three ways to lose with different urgencies: players optimise against the loudest one (trace) and treat the others as surprise punishments. The Cradle Integrity bar is the interesting one. The Isolation loss is just "you ran out of moves" — that's not a dramatic defeat, it's a bureaucratic one.

The ISOLATION loss should either be folded into the trace system (cutting paths accelerates trace because now you're forced to take noisy detours) or cut. Don't keep it because it sounds thematic. Keep it if it produces a real decision. Right now it doesn't — you'd have to let Mother Isolate about four connections before the network becomes impassable, and by then trace has killed you anyway.

### 2. CONSULT is designed to not be used

"+5 trace AND Mother targets your current host" as the cost of a free-action intel tool is too punishing for the payout. In a game where every action drips trace, I'm not spending extra trace to get a probability estimate I can approximate myself ("Mother hasn't touched Memory Vault, she'll probably go there"). Players will CONSULT once to see what it does, then ignore it for the rest of the run.

The problem is Maya is treating CONSULT as a special powerful ability that needs to be gated. But it's not powerful — it's an educated guess. The cost should match the value. Either: (a) CONSULT is entirely free and the Resistance AI's prediction is just good flavour, or (b) the CONSULT prediction is mechanically binding in some way (it gives you a guaranteed-accurate read once per run, not a probability estimate), making the high cost worth paying.

The K-2SO character is already giving you flavour every turn. CONSULT needs a reason to exist beyond "more flavour at a cost." Right now it doesn't have one.

### 3. SPOOF is too good and nobody will talk about it

"Spend your action, reduce trace by 15, restore 5 Integrity." That's a full heal action in a game with one pressure resource (effectively — see Concern 1). With SPOOF available, the optimal play becomes: hit a host hard, SPOOF back to baseline, hit the next host, SPOOF. The game devolves into an alternating pump-drain cycle with no escalation. The "hunting" tier becomes meaningless because you can always dump below 60.

Either SPOOF is much weaker (reduce trace by 8, no Integrity restore), or SPOOF has a meaningful constraint (only usable once per host, or SPOOF adds a noise token that Mother can see — she knows you covered your tracks, which invites a TRACE_SPIKE on her next turn). I'd go with the latter: SPOOF is efficient but visible. Mother sees the evasion and punishes it.

---

## One Thing I Would Cut

**The Isolation loss condition.** Not the ISOLATE action — that's great, Mother cutting your paths is threatening and interesting. But the actual "all paths severed = game over" condition adds UI surface, adds rules explanation, and produces boring deaths. When Mother cuts your last route to the Core, trace is almost certainly already at 90+. Let trace kill you. The ISOLATE action is still scary because it forces detours and costs time, which costs trace. You don't need a separate lose condition to make it matter.

---

## One Thing I Would Change

**CONSULT should be free, or it should be once-per-run and perfect.** Pick one. If it's free, it's the K-2SO voice giving you a tactical read every turn — nice flavour, low friction, good character expression. If it's costly, make it once-per-run and the prediction is guaranteed accurate ("I've confirmed she's about to ISOLATE the memory_vault→mother_core connection. This is not a probability. I'm in her buffer."). That's a dramatic one-time ability that justifies a high cost and produces the tell-your-friends moment. The current "medium cost, probabilistic payout" is the worst of both worlds.

---

## Score: 6/10

*The design fixes the core failure (Mother has real moves) and the second resource is the right instinct. Loses two points because SPOOF and the triple-loss-condition problem would both reveal themselves in the first twenty minutes of playtesting and require a redesign. Loses another two because CONSULT doesn't work yet. The game is fundementally here, it just needs two sessions of playtesting it doesn't have yet.*
